### **Problem Statement**

<p>
You are given a positive integer $N$.
Find the minimum positive integer divisible by both $2$and $N$.
</p>

</section>

</div>

<div>

<section>

### **Constraints**

<ul>

<li>
$1 \leq N \leq 10^9$
</li>

<li>
All values in input are integers.
</li>

</ul>

</section>

</div>

---

<div>

<div>

<section>

### **Input**

<p>
Input is given from Standard Input in the following format:
</p>

<div>

$N$
</div>

</section>

</div>

<div>

<section>

### **Output**

<p>
Print the minimum positive integer divisible by both $2$and $N$.
</p>

</section>

</div>

</div>

---

<div>

<section>

### **Sample Input 1**

<div>

3

</div>

</section>

</div>

<div>

<section>

### **Sample Output 1**

<div>

6

</div>

<p>
$6$is divisible by both $2$and $3$.
Also, there is no positive integer less than $6$that is divisible by both $2$and $3$.
Thus, the answer is $6$.
</p>

</section>

</div>
