<section>

### **Problem Statement**

<p>
You are given a positive integer $X$.
Find the largest 
<em>
perfect power
</em>
that is at most $X$.
Here, a perfect power is an integer that can be represented as $b^p$, where $b$is an integer not less than $1$and $p$is an integer not less than $2$.
</p>

</section>

</div>

<div>

<section>

### **Constraints**

<ul>

<li>
$1$$≤$$X$$≤$$1000$
</li>

<li>
$X$is an integer.
</li>

</ul>

</section>

</div>

---

<div>

<div>

<section>

### **Input**

<p>
Input is given from Standard Input in the following format:
</p>

<div>

$X$
</div>

</section>

</div>

<div>

<section>

### **Output**

<p>
Print the largest perfect power that is at most $X$.
</p>

</section>

</div>

</div>

---

<div>

<section>

### **Sample Input 1**

<div>

10

</div>

</section>

</div>

<div>

<section>

### **Sample Output 1**

<div>

9

</div>

<p>
There are four perfect powers that are at most $10$: $1$, $4$, $8$and $9$.
We should print the largest among them, $9$.
</p>

</section>
